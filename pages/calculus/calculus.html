The Idea Behind Calculus
Introduction

Let's say that we're trying to find the area of a circle. We can divide the circle into rings. The length of each of these rectangular rings is \(2\pi r\) and the thickness depends on how finely we chop up the circle. Let's call this thickness \(dr\), the difference in radius from one ring to the next.

We can place the rectangles together and form a triangle. We can then calculate the area of the triangles, as we know that the base length is the same as the radius of the original circle and the height is the length of the longest ring, \(2\pi r\).

\[A=\frac{1}{2}bh=\frac{1}{2}r2\pi r=\pi r^2\]

We arrive at the formula for the area of a circle.
